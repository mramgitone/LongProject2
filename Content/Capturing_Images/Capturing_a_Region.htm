Capturing a Region

Use this method to capture a rectangular region of your computer screen. You can draw a rectangle over any area of your computer screen and capture that region. This is useful, for example, if you want to capture only a portion of a toolbar or a specific area of a window, but not the entire window.

how to capture a region
  1. (Optional)

    • Ribbon In the New Screen Capture Options section, click the down arrow next to the Current Profile field.

    • Standard Toolbar Click the down arrow next to the Current Profile field.
  2. Open the window(s) with regions that you want to capture and/or arrange your desktop exactly as needed. When you initiate a region capture, the Capture application will be minimized, allowing you to capture a region for whatever is directly behind it.
  3. Open Capture.
    • Ribbon Select File>New Screen Capture.
    • Menu Select Capture>Capture Region.
    • Keyboard Shortcut Press CTRL+SHIFT+R.

    Intersecting red "crosshairs" are displayed on your computer screen.

  5. Move your cursor to the location where you would like to begin the captured region (e.g., the upper-left corner of the prospective region). As you move the cursor, a small window displays the vertical and horizontal position of the cursor (in pixels) on the screen.

  6. Click the left mouse button and drag to draw a rectangle, releasing the button when you are satisfied with the region (identified by red borders and a small window displaying the size of the rectangle in pixels). As soon as you release the mouse button, the Capture task bar is displayed next to the drawn region.

  7. The image is captured and opens in the Capture Editor.
 notes

If you are working on a project in MadCap Flare and want to capture and insert an image, you can initiate a Region capture from within Flare by selecting Insert>Screen Capture.

You can also perform a Region capture, inserting the image as an object on top of an existing image. See Inserting Captured Regions as Objects.
