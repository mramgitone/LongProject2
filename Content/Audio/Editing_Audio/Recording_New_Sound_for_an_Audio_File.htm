Recording New Sound for an Audio File

After you record an audio file, you can edit the file. One way to do this is to record additional audio and insert it into the existing audio file.

how to record new sound for an audio file
  1. Make sure a microphone is connected to your computer.
  2. Open the movie.
  3. Open one of the following, depending on the element for which you have recorded audio.
    • The properties dialog for an object (double-click the object)
    • The Frame Properties dialog (double-click the frame)
    • The Movie Properties dialog (select Movie>Properties in either the ribbon or menu interface)
  4. Select the Audio tab. The recorded audio file should be displayed in the Audio field.
  5. Click Edit. The Sound Editor opens.

  6. Click once in the display where you want to insert the new audio.

    When you click in the display, the playhead (a red vertical line) is shown. The new audio will be inserted immediately after the playhead.

  7. Click . The Record dialog opens.
  8. In the Input Source field, select the appropriate source.
  9. Click Record.
  10. Speak into the microphone.
  11. When you are finished, click Stop.
  12. In the Record dialog, click OK.
  13. In the Sound Editor, click OK.
  14. In the properties dialog, click OK.
