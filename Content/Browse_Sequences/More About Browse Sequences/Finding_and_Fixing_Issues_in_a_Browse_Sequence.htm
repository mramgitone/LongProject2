Finding and Fixing Issues in a Browse Sequence

You can use buttons in the local toolbar of a browse sequence to navigate to the next warning and previous warning. This helps you to find and then fix issues (e.g., broken links) in the browse sequence.

how to find and fix issues in a browse sequence
  1. Open the browse sequence. If the browse sequence contains unlinked items or broken links, it will be indicated in the local toolbar.
  2. Click to find and highlight the next issue in the browse sequence. To move to the previous issue, you can click .
  3. After you find an issue, you can take action, such as removing the item or clicking to open the properties where you can link the item to a file.

What if you do not want it to find the unlinked books, but rather the broken items only? In that case, simply click the Show Unlinked Books button . This hides the unlinked book icons. Therefore, when you click the Find Previous Warning or Find Next Warning button, it will skip over the unlinked books and move to the previous or next broken item.
