Segmentation Rules and Spaces

For more information, see About Segmentation Rules.

example—do not break

Let’s say you have the date Feb. 21 (with a space between) and want to make sure it appears in the same segment. Without a segmentation rule, Lingo will break the segment after Feb. because it thinks it is the end of a sentence.

You can create a rule by entering Feb. in the After cell and 21 in the Before cell. However, when you enter 21 in the Before cell, make sure you also add a space before it. Otherwise, the segment will break only if it comes across Feb.21 (with no space) but not if it comes across Feb. 21 (with a space).
