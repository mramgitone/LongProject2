Adding Blur Effects

example

example

Here is an example without the blur effect.

We've drawn a rectangle around the word "madcap." Then we applied an effect to blur the rest of the image around this rectangle.

You can quickly add a blur effect with a rectangle shape by using the "Blur Effect Mode." Otherwise, you can use a slightly longer process and add a blur effect for any shape.

how to use the blur effect mode for a rectangle
  1. Open a frame.

    • Ribbon  In the Editing section, click .

    • Menu Select Insert>Effect>Blur Effect Mode.
    • Local Toolbar Do the following.
      1. In the local toolbar, click the arrow next to the Effect button .
      2. From the drop-down list, select Blur Effect Mode.

    The cursor changes to small crosshairs.

  3. Click in the frame and drag the mouse to draw the rectangle over the area of the frame that you want to remain clear. Release the mouse button when you are finished. The rectangle appears on the frame and the area around it is blurred.

  4. If necessary, you can make modifications to the effect, including the following.

    modify the amount of blur applied around the rectangle
    1. Double-click the frame (not the shape).
    2. In the Frame Properties dialog, select the Image Effects tab.
    3. Change the number in the Blur Factor field. The higher the number, the more the frame is blurred.
         modify the look of the rectangle
    1. Double-click the shape (not the frame).
    2. In the properties dialog for the object, use the various tabs to make changes to the rectangle. To remove the border around the rectangle, select the Appearance tab and set the Line:Width field to 0.
         move or resize the rectangle
    • To move the entire rectangle, click somewhere in the middle of the rectangle and drag it to the new location.
    • To resize the rectangle, click any of the points (i.e., small circles) around the edge of the rectangle and drag it to adjust the height and/or width.
how to add a blur effect for any shape
  1. Open a frame.

  2. In the local toolbar of the Frame Editor, click the appropriate shape button:

  3. Click in the frame and draw the shape over the area of the frame that you want to remain clear.

    For more information about how to draw each shape, see page Adding Shapes to Frames.

    note

    If you draw a text box, you'll also need to provide text for the shape and then click outside of it.

  4. Double-click the shape (not the frame). The properties dialog for the object opens.

  5. Select the Image Effects tab.
  6. Click the check box next to Enable Blur Effect.

  7. The area around the shape is blurred.

  8. If necessary, you can make modifications to the effect, including the following.

    modify the amount of blur applied around the shape
    1. Double-click the frame (not the shape).
    2. In the Frame Properties dialog, select the Image Effects tab.
    3. Change the number in the Blur Factor field. The higher the number, the more the frame is blurred.
         modify the look of the shape
    1. Double-click the shape (not the frame).
    2. In the properties dialog for the object, use the various tabs to make changes to the shape.
    move or resize the shape
    • To move the entire shape, click somewhere in the middle of the shape and drag it to the new location.
    • To resize the shape, click any of the points (i.e., small circles) around the edge of the shape and drag it to adjust the height and/or width.
notes

If you want to blur the area within a shape, instead of around it, you can apply a blur-inside effect. See Adding Blur-Inside Effects.
